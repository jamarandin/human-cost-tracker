The data

How reported, confirmed, and verified differ

"Reported" means a credible source published the figure. "Confirmed" means a rights monitor verified who was killed and their status. If Reuters reports more than 1,900 deaths in Iran while HRANA confirms 1,616 civilian deaths, both are true—it means 284 deaths are still being classified.

Injuries (20,000+) vastly outnumber deaths (1,900). We show them at different scales so you can see both clearly.

The sources and their methodologies

Reuters, HRANA, and official counts do not always match because they have different access, different methods, and sometimes count different things. "Reported" means a credible source has published the figure. "Confirmed" means a monitor or rights group has verified who was killed. If Reuters reports more than 1,900 killed in Iran while HRANA confirms 1,616 civilian deaths, that does not mean the difference is false. It means some deaths have not yet been fully classified.
